





Bloodborne Pathogen Exposure Control



Policy



Unit Drilling will provide hand-washing facilities or antiseptic towelettes for cleanup after any potential exposure to bloodborne and other pathogens.



Unit Drilling personnel are not required to render first aid or CPR, unless the performance of first aid is part of an employee’s designated job duty, or the employee is participating in a special emergency medical response organization.



Each rig and facility will provide and maintain



--	three <a href="https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1910/1910.1030">29 CFR 1910.1030(d)(3)(i)</a> – compliant First Aid or dedicated Bloodborne Pathogens kits

--	leak-proof bags for disposal of potentially infected clothing and PPE

--	biohazard labels to mark disposal bags and other potentially infected areas, materials, and equipment.



Training



Unit Personnel expected to provide first aid will be trained per the <a href="https://unitcorp.kpaehs.com/rsc/67c4bf36d7480a0012d5a2c4">CPR and First Aid Preparedness</a> procedure.



Hepatitis B



Unit Drilling will pay for Hepatitis-B vaccination for



--	any employee expected to provide first aid

--	any employee involved in an exposure incident

--	any previously unvaccinated employee who has rendered assistance in any situation involving the presence of blood or other potentially infectious material



Unit personnel who decline this vaccination will sign a vaccine declination form.



The HSE Manager will retain vaccination records (these will only state whether a vaccine was administered or for some reason counter indicated) and declination forms for 30 years.



Procedure



After an Exposure Incident



Per <a href="https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1910/1910.1030">29 CFR 1910.1030</a>, Exposure Incident means a specific eye, mouth, other mucous membrane, non-intact skin, or parenteral [entering the body by means other than ingestion] contact with blood or other potentially infectious materials that results from the performance of an employee's duties.



1)	The exposed person will

	a)	Report exposure to the Rig Manager.

	b)	Remove and disinfect or dispose of any potentially infected clothing and PPE.

	c)	Thoroughly wash any exposed parts of the body.

2)	The Rig Manager will

	a)	Where necessary, create and carry out a plan, including JSA, for disinfecting potentially infected areas and equipment.

	b)	Complete a Supervisor's First Report of Injury <a href="https://unitcorp.kpaehs.com/forms">SAF-RIG-6.1</a>.

	c)	Notify the HSE Manager.

3)	The HSE Manager will

	a)	Contact the exposed person to offer a confidential evaluation and follow-up with a healthcare professional.
