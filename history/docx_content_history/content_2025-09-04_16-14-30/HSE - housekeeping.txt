Housekeeping



Policy



Provisioning



Each rig or facility that may produce oily rags, waste oil, or used oil filters will have a leak-proof, air-tight container for segregating and storing waste oil and waste-oil contaminated materials. Label this container “used oil”.



Each rig or facility that may produce used fuel filters will have a leak-proof, air-tight container for segregating and storing waste fuel and waste-fuel contaminated materials. Label this container “used fuel filters”.



Each rig or facility that may need to store more than 25 gallons of paint or flammable materials will supply a <a href="https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1910/1910.106">29 CFR 1910.106</a> compatible flammables cabinet.



General Requirements



Dispose of waste oil, waste hydraulic fluid, oily rags, and fuel filters in designated waste-oil containers.



Dispose of used fuel filters in designated “used fuel filters” containers.



Do not store materials on tops of lockers.



Segregate scrap metal.



Keep walkways and stairways clear.



Do not block or inhibit access to fire extinguishers, eyewash stations, electrical panels, BOP controls, or other emergency equipment or supplies.



When drilling, clean any spilled mud as soon as practicable after each connection.



Dispose of caustic sacks in closed plastic trash bags before placing them in a dumpster.
