Safety Signs



Policy



Rig Sites



Rigs will display safety signs per the <a href="https://unitcorp.kpaehs.com/rsc/67c4bf36d7480a0012d5a2e2">Safety Signs Rig Map</a> along with any additional signs required to meet the requirement for “other facilities”.



Other Facilities



Conform to <a href="https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1910/1910.145">CFR 29 1910.145</a> standards for safety signs. In general:



--	Red Danger Signs to indicate immediate danger

--	Yellow or Orange Caution Signs or Warning Signs to warn against potential hazards, caution against unsafe practices, and indicate equipment that may start automatically

--	Green Safety Instruction Signs to indicate first aid stations, eye wash stations, chemical showers, and other safety equipment or supplies

--	Blue Notice Signs to provide general information or instructions unrelated to personal safety

--	Fire Safety Signs to indicate the location of fire alarms, fire extinguishers, emergency exits, and evacuation routes.



Conform to <a href="https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1910/1910.37">29 CFR 1910.37</a> standards for exit signs. In general:



--	Mark each exit with an EXIT sign.

--	Mark non-exit doors along exit routes with “not an exit”.



Cover these specific requirements:



--	A “face-shield required” sign near all abrasive-wheel machinery.

--	A “hearing protection required” sign where noise levels exceed 82 decibels for 12-hour shifts or 85 decibels for 8-hour shifts.
