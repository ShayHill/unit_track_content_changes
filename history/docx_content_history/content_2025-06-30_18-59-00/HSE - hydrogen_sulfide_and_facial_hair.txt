Hydrogen Sulfide and Facial Hair



Policy



All Drilling Sites



All drilling sites will have a fixed H2S monitoring system.



H2S alarms will meet the requirements of <a href="https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1910/1910.165">29 CFR 1910.165(b)</a>.



H2S alarms will be set to alarm at 10ppm.



Install windsocks that are clearly visible to all personnel on location.



Sites Suspected to Contain H2S



Personnel will wear a personal H2s monitor.



Personnel will complete a documented annual H2S training course.



Mark site access points to warn personnel of potential H2S exposure.



Personnel will be supplied with supplied-air respirators. These will meet the requirement of <a href="https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1910/1910.134">29 CFR 1910.134(d)(2) Respirators for IDLH atmospheres</a>.



Respirators



The client or operator will supply and maintain supplied-air respirators for drilling operations. For any supplied-air respirators owned by Unit Drilling:



Inspect supplied-air respirators monthly.

--	Inspect air cylinders monthly.

--	Store in designated storage containers.

--	Keep fully charged and ready if intended for emergency use.



Facial Hair



Personnel who are



--	Essential to rig operations; and

--	foreseeably expected to wear a face mask



will be clean shaven where the face mask would seal against the face.



Working Around H2S



Unit Drilling personnel will not enter a confined space with > 0 ppm H2S.



Unit Drilling personnel will not work in any atmosphere greater than 0 and <10ppm for more than 30 minutes without a supplied-air breathing apparatus meeting the requirements of <a href="https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1910/1910.134">29 CFR 1910.134(d)(2) Respirators for IDLH atmospheres</a>.



Unit Drilling personnel will not work in any atmosphere >10 ppm H2S without a supplied-air breathing apparatus meeting the requirements of 29 CFR 1910.134(d)(2) Respirators for IDLH atmospheres.



Unit Drilling personnel will not work in any atmosphere >50ppm H2S.
